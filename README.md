Sturij Marketing Platform
Sturij's Facebook-group marketing, made repeatable and safe — without losing what makes it work: Polly, and the local trust she's built over years.

What it is
The platform captures how the marketing really works and turns it into tools that help Polly do it faster and safer — never tools that act in her name.

Discovery — a short debrief (polly_debrief_form.html) that captures how Polly reads a group and where enquiries actually come from. A calibration instrument, not a census — it teaches the system the pattern, it isn't filled 325 times.
Group Ledger — rules + risk per group (green / amber / red). Seeded automatically from the groups export, then Polly confirms, never composes. It stays living: it learns from what actually happens (a post that stuck, a mod that reacted), not from more forms.
Survival playbook — the do's and don'ts, distilled.
Reply tool — drafts DMs and comments in Polly's voice. She reads, tweaks, sends.
The one principle (read this twice)
The tool DRAFTS. Polly always SENDS. Nothing posts or messages on its own — ever. Flow is always propose → Polly approves → send. One bad autonomous post can lose a group she spent years earning trust in. The tools make her safer and more in control, not less.

Orientation
CLAUDE.md — the working rules for any AI/human session here. Read first. Binding.
STURIJ_DESIGN.md — the look (fonts, palette, feel). High-level control; match it, keep it simple — no token system, no build machinery.
polly_debrief_form.html — the discovery form and the reference for how the design looks in practice.
strategy_framework/ — discovery inputs, the ledger, the playbook.
Status
Early setup. Structure and rules first; surfaces designed against STURIJ_DESIGN.md; the reply tool and ledger come once discovery is in.

Relationship to the main Sturij app
Separate repo, its own simple skin for now — but it shares the design DNA and the propose → approve → send grammar with the main platform, so the two can converge later if this works well. That convergence is a merge, not a rebuild — keep to both.
